Radium

« Back to element


Protons: 88
Neutrons: 138
Electrons: 88




Radiation is a natural process that allows an atom to reach a stable state. Radium releases helium atoms to become stable.














Radium is a silvery-white color. It quickly turns black when it reacts with nitrogen in the air.
